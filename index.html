Questions

Things people
ask us.

Does a Red signal mean the product doesn't work?
No — and this is the most important thing to understand about NORM. A Red signal means a brand made strong claims without showing you the evidence to back them up on their page. The product might work brilliantly. The ingredient might have decades of research behind it. But right now, on this page, they're asking you to take their word for it. That's worth knowing before you spend $80 a month.
So what does NORM actually tell me?
It measures the gap between what a brand claims and what evidence they visibly connect to those claims. Green means claims appear proportionate to available evidence — support exists and is clearly linked. Yellow means evidence exists, but is not clearly connected to all claims — gaps are present. Red means claims extend beyond visible support — the gap between what is claimed and what is shown is material. NORM does not tell you whether to buy. It shows what holds.
Can a brand pay to improve their signal?
No. Scores are determined solely by what's visible on the consumer-facing product page at time of scan. The Brand Transparency plan ($199/month) shows brands what's driving their signal and lets them submit evidence for review — but paying for that has zero effect on their score. The only path to a better signal is making stronger evidence visible on the actual page consumers visit.
How is the score calculated?
The Claim Support Risk score (0–100) runs across six dimensions: Evidence Alignment (25%), Claim Clarity (15%), Evidence Quality (15%), Consumer Distortion Risk (15%), Claim Strength (10%), and Evidence Accessibility (10%). Each claim is also weighted by where it appears — hero headlines carry 3× more weight than footer disclaimers, because that's where brands put the claims they most want you to believe. High-intensity language like "clinically proven" with no visible evidence carries an additional penalty. The complete methodology is published at trynorm.co/methodology.
I'm a brand with a Red signal — what do I do?
The signal reflects what's visible on your page right now. The fastest path to a better signal is making your evidence more visible — add links to published studies, display your third-party testing certificates, disclose ingredient dosages clearly. Our Brand Transparency plan ($199/month) shows you exactly which dimensions are driving your score so you know where to focus. We also accept evidence submissions for human review at any time.
