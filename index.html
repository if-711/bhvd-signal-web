Questions

Things people
ask us.

Does a Red signal mean the product doesn't work?
No — and this is the most important thing to understand about NORM. A Red signal means a brand made strong claims without showing you the evidence to back them up on their page. The product might work brilliantly. The ingredient might have decades of research behind it. But right now, on this page, they're asking you to take their word for it. That's worth knowing before you spend $80 a month.
Can a brand pay to improve their signal?
No. Scores are determined solely by what's visible on the consumer-facing product page at time of scan. The Brand Portal lets brands see what's driving their signal and submit evidence for review — but none of that has any effect on their score. The only path to a better signal is making stronger evidence visible on the actual page consumers visit.
How is the score calculated?
The NORM score (0–100) runs across six dimensions: Evidence Strength (25 pts), Consumer Distortion (20 pts), Specificity (15 pts), Mechanism Integrity (15 pts), Effect Reality (15 pts), and Evidence Presence (10 pts). Each claim is weighted by where it appears — hero headlines count more than footer disclaimers, because that's where brands put the claims they most want you to believe. The complete methodology is published at trynorm.co/methodology.
