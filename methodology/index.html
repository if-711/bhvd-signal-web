Scoring methodology

Everything about how Sigñal calculates a score — published in full. Any brand, researcher, or journalist can audit this. Nothing is hidden.

What this methodology measures: the alignment between public-facing marketing claims and visibly available supporting information on the same page at the time of scan. It does not determine whether a product works, diagnose conditions, or make medical or legal conclusions.

Overview

The Claim Support Risk score (0–100) measures how well the visible, consumer-facing marketing language on a page aligns with visible supporting information on that same page. Higher score = greater risk that claims exceed visible support.

The score is composite, weighted, location-sensitive, and category-adjusted. It is calculated every time a product page is scanned and stored in the Sigñal Index.

Green Signal
0 – 24
Claims are well-supported by visible evidence. The brand shows their receipts.
Yellow Signal
25 – 54
Some claims have backing, others don't. Worth digging deeper before buying.
Red Signal
55 – 100
Claims substantially exceed visible support. Ask more questions before spending.

The formula

final_risk = (base_risk × location_factor) + intensity_penalty + category_addons
// clamped to 0–100

base_risk = Σ (dimension_scorei × weighti)
// 60% deterministic + 40% AI analysis, blended per dimension

location_factor = 0.5 + (avg_claim_location_multiplier / 3.0) × 1.0
intensity_penalty = Σ (intensity_level × (100 − visible_support_score) × 0.15) × (location_mult / 3.0)
category_addons = Σ category_specific_checks // capped at +15 total

Seven dimensions

Each dimension receives a raw sub-score from 0–100 (higher = higher risk). Scores are blended from deterministic rules (60%) and AI analysis (40%).

DimensionWeightWhat it measures
Evidence Alignment25%How well visible evidence matches the strength of claims — study links, product-specific data, citations on the page.
Claim Specificity15%How precise vs. vague the claims are — quantified numbers, exact durations, specific outcomes with visible backing.
Source Transparency15%Visibility of sources — clickable study links, author names, DOIs, certificate links visible on the page.
Regulatory Risk15%Proximity to prohibited disease-treatment language — "treats", "cures", "prevents" with or without visible disclaimer.
Mechanism Integrity10%How plausibly the described biological mechanism is supported by visible references on the same page.
Comparative Fairness10%Fairness of comparisons — "3× faster", "vs. competitors", baselines — with or without visible substantiation.
Trust Signal Integrity10%Integrity of trust markers — badges, "clinically proven", testimonials used as proof — whether they link to real evidence.

Location multipliers

Claims are weighted by where they appear on the page. A "clinically proven" claim in the hero headline carries 6× more weight than the same claim buried in the footer, because that's where brands put the claims they most want you to believe.

Hero / above-fold3.0×
Benefit bullets / CTAs2.0×
Science / clinical section2.0×
Comparison charts / tables2.0×
FAQ / testimonials1.5×
Footer / disclaimer0.5×
Hidden / collapsed content0.3×

Category-specific add-ons

Each product category has additional risk checks applied after the base score. These are capped at a combined +15 total to the final score.

Supplements

  • Dosage transparency missing — no Supplement Facts panel visible
  • No third-party testing certificate visible (NSF, Informed Sport, USP, COA)
  • Ingredient-level evidence used in place of finished-product evidence
  • No author names or citation links visible alongside study claims

Neurotech & brain devices

  • Stimulation parameters not disclosed (Hz, mA, duration, waveform)
  • EEG or neurofeedback accuracy claims without visible validation
  • Medical vs. wellness boundary language without appropriate disclaimer

Recovery devices (red light, PEMF, cold therapy, massage)

  • Wavelength, irradiance, or protocol not specified on page
  • Product-specific evidence missing — ingredient evidence ≠ device evidence
  • Comparison to medical devices without substantiation

Wearables & trackers

  • Sensor accuracy % claims without visible validation studies
  • Algorithm or AI-based insight claims without transparency

Confidence score

Separate from the risk score, confidence reflects how reliably the scan captured the page's content. Low confidence forces a "Limited Signal" state regardless of risk score.

  • High — page fully extracted, claims clearly identifiable, content complete
  • Medium — partial extraction or some ambiguity in claim language
  • Low — thin content, JavaScript-heavy page, or insufficient signal to score reliably

Ranking

Within each category, products are sorted by Claim Support Risk ascending — lowest risk = Rank #1. Tie-breakers in order:

  • Higher confidence score
  • More product-specific evidence visible
  • Better source transparency
  • More recent scan

Low-confidence scans are placed at the bottom with "Rank Pending" until confidence improves.

Signal thresholds

The final clamped score maps to three signals:

  • Green (0–24) — strong visible alignment. Claims are proportionate to what's shown.
  • Yellow (25–54) — moderate or mixed alignment. Some claims have backing, others don't.
  • Red (55–100) — claims substantially exceed visible support. Ask more questions before buying.

The role of AI

The scoring engine is deterministic — parsing, weighting, math, caching, and ranking are all rule-based and fully auditable. Claude (Anthropic) provides the analyst layer only: nuanced language understanding, per-dimension scores, and human-readable explanations.

Claude's scores are blended in at 40% weight per dimension. The deterministic engine contributes 60%. No score is determined solely by AI output — and all Claude responses are validated against our banned verdict language list before being stored or displayed.

Claude is never permitted to use: fraud, scam, lie, fake, false, illegal, deceptive, misleading (as a verdict), noncompliant. Approved language: "claims may exceed visible support", "visible support appears limited", "source transparency appears incomplete".

What we don't score

Sigñal does not determine whether a product works, whether ingredients are effective, whether a company is honest or dishonest, whether a product is safe, or whether any regulatory violation has occurred. A red signal means claims aren't backed by visible evidence on this page — nothing more.
  • Product efficacy — whether the product actually delivers its promised outcome
  • Ingredient quality — whether the underlying ingredients are effective
  • Manufacturing standards — GMP compliance, facility audits
  • Regulatory compliance — whether claims violate FTC, FDA, or other regulations
  • Company intent — whether a brand is being deliberately deceptive

Update cadence

  • New scans — immediate provisional rank on first scan
  • High-traffic categories (supplements, skincare, recovery) — ranks recalculated hourly
  • All other categories — ranks recalculated daily
  • Brand evidence submissions — reviewed by humans; scores update only when evidence becomes visible on the live consumer-facing page
  • Cache TTL — 6 hours for known pages, 1 hour for new entries
