How We Score

We measure whether a brand's marketing is proportional to its visible evidence — not whether the product works.

NORM scores the gap between what a brand claims and what it publicly shows to back those claims. A strong score means the brand is specific, cites real evidence, and doesn't overreach. A weak score means the marketing runs ahead of what the brand actually shows — not necessarily that the product doesn't work.

What we look at

Every brand is evaluated on six dimensions: how specific the claims are, whether the brand explains the mechanism behind them, whether evidence is present and linked, how strong that evidence is, whether claimed effects match published science, and whether the marketing language is proportionate to what's actually shown.

Verdicts

Aligned

Claims are specific, grounded, and backed by visible evidence.

Validated

Strong evidence base with minor gaps.

Generally Aligned

Reasonable foundation with some unsupported areas.

Partially Aligned

Some claims are supported, but key marketing language goes beyond what's shown.

Overextended

Claims materially exceed the visible evidence.

Evidence Gap

Marketing is not meaningfully supported by anything the brand makes publicly visible.

Independence

Brands cannot pay for inclusion, better scores, or faster review. Payment never influences ratings. Scores are editorial assessments — not FDA approval, health endorsements, or legal clearance of any kind.

Scope

We review publicly visible brand websites and product pages only. Scores reflect a point-in-time snapshot and are periodically refreshed as brands update their pages.
