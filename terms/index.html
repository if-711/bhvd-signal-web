Plain language summary: NORM scores how well brands show evidence for their marketing claims — it is not medical advice, not a product safety assessment, and not a statement about whether any product works. Scores are editorial opinions about visible marketing materials, nothing more.

1. Who we are

NORM is operated by NORM Analytics ("NORM", "we", "us"). We publish an editorial index that evaluates the degree to which wellness, nutrition, and performance brands substantiate their public marketing claims with visible evidence on their consumer-facing pages.

By accessing trynorm.co or any NORM service, you agree to these terms.

2. What NORM scores are — and are not

NORM scores are editorial assessments of visible marketing claims and evidence. They are opinions about the relationship between a brand's public claims and the evidence they publicly show to support those claims. They are not:

  • Medical or health advice of any kind
  • Statements about whether any product is effective, safe, or appropriate for any person
  • FDA review, regulatory assessment, or legal clearance
  • Guarantee of product quality, purity, or performance
  • Endorsement or recommendation to purchase or avoid any product

A low NORM score means a brand made strong marketing claims without showing visible evidence to support them — it does not mean the product doesn't work. A high score means a brand showed their proof publicly — it does not mean the product is safe or effective for you.

Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before making decisions about supplements, health products, or wellness interventions.

3. Scores change

Scores reflect a point-in-time assessment of public brand pages. As brands update their marketing materials, pages are re-evaluated and scores change. A current score may differ materially from a historical score. We do not guarantee that any score will remain current, and historical scores should not be relied upon as a current representation of a brand's practices.

Reviewers do not have access to commercial account information when scoring. This separation is structural.
